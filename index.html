Our Network

A regional food system built on partnership

SFUA works with farms, schools, food banks, universities, and public agencies across Southern California. Each partner plays a distinct role in the regional food system, and each makes the work of the others more effective.

The Case for Collaboration

Strong food systems are built together

No single organization can build a resilient regional food system on its own. The work requires growers who know their land, educators who understand their students, distributors with the logistics to move fresh food efficiently, and institutions with the community trust to reach households that need it most.

SFUA's role is to coordinate and strengthen the relationships between these organizations. We do not seek to replace or absorb our partners. We invest in the infrastructure, knowledge, and connections that allow each part of the network to function more effectively as a whole.

A regional food system is not a single organization. It is a web of relationships, and it grows stronger every time a new partner joins.

Over more than a decade of work in Southern California, SFUA has found that the most durable programs are built in genuine collaboration. The farms that have stayed in the network longest are those where trust developed over time. The school programs that produce lasting change are those where educators and SFUA staff designed the work together. Partnership is not just a structure. It is how the work gets done.

How We Work Together

Partnerships take different forms

SFUA engages partners through several distinct relationship structures. The model for each partnership depends on the site, the institutional context, and what each organization brings to the collaboration. In all cases, SFUA contributes agricultural expertise, food system coordination, and regional network connections.

Directly Operated
🌿

Directly Operated Programs

At select sites, SFUA takes a direct operational role. SFUA manages the farm or program, leads day-to-day operations, and is responsible for outcomes. These sites anchor the broader network and demonstrate what is possible when all elements of the food system are coordinated.

SFUA's Role Day-to-day operations, staffing, production management, and programming. Full operational responsibility.
Co-Operated
🤝

Programs Co-Operated with Partners

At institutional sites such as schools, municipalities, and community facilities, SFUA co-operates programs alongside the host organization. The institution provides land, community access, and local context. SFUA contributes agricultural knowledge and food system connections.

SFUA's Role Shared operations, production or curriculum leadership, and food system integration on partner-owned land.
Partner-Led
🏠

Partner-Led Initiatives

Many farms and programs in the SFUA network are operated independently by growers and community organizations. SFUA supports these sites with coordination services, market access, and connections to distribution and education partners across the region.

SFUA's Role Market coordination, technical assistance, shared infrastructure, and connection to regional distribution partners.
Regional Infrastructure
📈

Regional Infrastructure and Coordination

Beyond individual sites, SFUA maintains the connective infrastructure of the regional food system. This includes institutional buyer relationships, distribution logistics, cross-sector partnerships, and the regional coordination that no single farm or organization can sustain alone.

SFUA's Role Institutional relationships, logistics coordination, cross-sector convening, and regional food system planning.

What Partnership Provides

What organizations gain by working with us

SFUA brings together the relationships, expertise, and infrastructure that individual farms and organizations cannot build alone.

🌾

Agricultural Knowledge

Access to SFUA's experience in urban and peri-urban farming: soil and crop management, season planning, and sustainable growing practices suited to Southern California.

🚚

Expanded Distribution

Farm partners gain access to established distribution channels and relationships with food banks, school districts, and institutional buyers that would take years to build independently.

📚

Education Partnerships

Schools and universities gain structured farm-based learning experiences, curriculum resources, and connections to growers who are ready to welcome students onto working farms.

🌍

Community Visibility

Partners become part of a credible regional network, connecting with a broader community of practice working on the same food system challenges across Southern California.

📈

Regional Coordination

SFUA coordinates across county lines and across sectors. Partners benefit from relationships that span the full supply chain and from conversations that shape regional food policy and planning.

🌿

Shared Infrastructure

From cold storage coordination and distribution logistics to shared agricultural equipment and technical assistance, partners benefit from infrastructure that no single organization could build alone.

Where We Operate

A network larger than any single site

SFUA's active farm and program operations are concentrated in Orange County, Los Angeles County, and Riverside County. These three counties form the core of the regional network. Beyond this operational core, SFUA maintains food system partnerships and distribution relationships that extend across the broader Southern California region.

Farms in Orange County benefit from institutional buyers and food bank relationships in Los Angeles. Schools in Riverside County benefit from agricultural knowledge and curriculum resources developed at sites across the region. The value of the network comes from these connections, not from geography alone.

Orange County: Core farm operations, institutional land partnerships, and education programs
Los Angeles County: Urban farm network, food bank distribution partnerships, and school programs
Riverside County: Expanding farm and food system partnerships across the Inland region
