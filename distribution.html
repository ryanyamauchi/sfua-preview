Program Area

The infrastructure that moves food from farms to people

Growing food is only part of the equation. SFUA coordinates the distribution infrastructure that connects partner farms to food banks, school cafeterias, municipal programs, and community organizations across Southern California.

SFUA does not build parallel systems. It strengthens the organizations already serving their communities, connecting them to locally grown food at a scale small farms cannot achieve alone.

The Distribution Model

How food moves through the network

SFUA functions as the coordinating layer between growers and receivers — helping manage logistics, match supply with demand, and ensure produce reaches the organizations best positioned to distribute it.

🌾

Partner Farms

Farms across Orange, Los Angeles, and Riverside counties harvest produce on coordinated schedules. SFUA works with each farm on crop volumes, timing, and logistics.

→
●

SFUA Coordination

SFUA coordinates logistics across the network — working with farms on supply timing, supporting cold storage and transport, and matching available produce with distribution partners best positioned to use it.

→
🏠

Communities & Recipients

Fresh produce reaches food banks, school cafeterias, community distribution events, and direct-to-household programs — prioritizing neighborhoods with limited access to fresh food.

❓ Why coordination matters

Small farms rarely have the volume or relationships to serve institutional buyers alone. SFUA makes that scale possible.

☃ Cold chain and freshness

We maintain cold chain integrity from harvest through delivery so produce arrives at peak quality.

⚖ Equity-first routing

Routes are mapped against food insecurity data, prioritizing communities with the fewest alternatives.

Who We Reach

Where the food goes

SFUA's distribution work is directed toward communities where access to fresh, affordable produce is limited, by distance, income, or systemic underinvestment in local food infrastructure.

SFUA works with organizations already embedded in these communities: food banks, municipal programs, schools, clinics. It connects them to locally grown food and makes their existing work more effective.

🏠

Food-Insecure Households

Fresh produce reaches households that rely on emergency food systems for nutritional support.

🏫

School Cafeterias

Partner school districts receive locally grown produce, improving nutritional quality and local sourcing of student meals.

🪄

Senior & Healthcare Recipients

Healthcare and senior-serving partners integrate fresh food into medically informed care programs.

📍

Underserved Geographic Areas

Routes are mapped against food access data to prioritize communities with the fewest alternatives.
