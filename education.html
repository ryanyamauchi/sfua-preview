Program Area

Connecting people to where food comes from

SFUA connects students, educators, and communities to agricultural knowledge across Southern California, through farm visits, hands-on learning, school and youth partnerships, and educator engagement around food and farming. Understanding where food comes from is foundational to community health and to the long-term resilience of the regional food system.

What We Do

How SFUA delivers agricultural education

SFUA delivers agricultural education in three distinct contexts, each reaching a different audience with a shared purpose.

🏫

School Programs

SFUA partners with K–12 schools and youth programs to bring hands-on agricultural learning to students — through farm visits, agricultural pathway support, and educator engagement around food, farming, and where our food comes from.

Farm visits and hands-on agricultural field experiences
Agricultural pathway support, including FFA revival and program development
Curriculum connections linking agriculture to classroom learning
Educator and administrator engagement around food and farming
Youth leadership opportunities at farm sites and in the field
🏠

Community Education

Through farm tours, hands-on workshops, and community events, SFUA brings food and farming knowledge to adults, families, and neighborhood organizations across the region. Programming is multilingual and designed for a range of ages and backgrounds, grounded in local context.

Public farm tours and volunteer work days
Cooking and food preservation workshops
Home and community garden starter programs
Multilingual programming for diverse communities
Youth summer and after-school farm programs
🏫

University Partnerships

SFUA collaborates with universities and community colleges on applied research, student internships, and co-developed curriculum. These partnerships advance the field and build the next generation of urban agriculture practitioners and advocates.

Applied research partnerships on urban farming methods
Student internship and practicum placements
Co-developed curriculum for agriculture and food studies
Guest lectures and symposium participation
Joint grant applications and funded research projects
