How We Work

Different roles, shared goals

SFUA works through a range of farm relationships across the region. In some sites, we play a direct operating role. In others, we co-operate with institutional partners, including schools, municipalities, and community organizations, who steward the land. In still others, we support partner-led farms through coordination, market access, and shared infrastructure. The model varies by site; the goal is always the same.

🤝

Network Coordination

Partner farms bring their own leadership, methods, and growing knowledge. SFUA builds the connective tissue: linking farms to each other, to shared resources, and to the broader distribution network.

What this looks like Shared equipment access, group purchasing, collective harvest scheduling, and cross-farm knowledge sharing.
📈

Market Access & Connections

Small farms rarely have the volume or relationships to serve institutional buyers on their own. SFUA helps coordinate market relationships and distribution pathways, connecting partner farms with food banks, school districts, and community buyers.

What this looks like Coordinated delivery logistics, relationships with institutional buyers, and support for CSA and farmers market sales.
🌿

Sustainable Growing Standards

SFUA works with partner farms to adopt sustainable practices that protect soil health, conserve water, and ensure the long-term productivity of the land — across the network and within SFUA-operated sites.

What this looks like Composting, water-wise irrigation, organic pest management, and soil health monitoring across the network.

Where Farms Operate

Where we work today

SFUA's current farm network is active across three counties in the Los Angeles and Orange County metro region, with distribution and education work extending into Riverside County. This is our operational core, where SFUA has established relationships, infrastructure, and on-the-ground presence.

Los Angeles County

Urban and community farms · Largest network concentration

Orange County

Institutional and school-based farms · Community agriculture focus

Riverside County

Peri-urban and community farms · Distribution and education ties
