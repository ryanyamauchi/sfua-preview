About the Evening

A celebration rooted in this land

Every September, as the harvest season peaks in Orange County, SFUA gathers the people who make the regional food system possible: the farmers, educators, chefs, distributors, funders, and community members who believe fresh, locally grown food should be accessible to everyone.

Harvest Moon is SFUA's flagship annual event: an outdoor benefit dinner at Tanaka Farms in Irvine, surrounded by the working fields that embody our mission. The evening celebrates the farmers and partners who grow and distribute food across Southern California, and raises the resources that allow SFUA to continue and expand this work.

Good food. Good people. One evening to celebrate what we have built and invest in what we are still growing.

This year's theme, Rooted in Orange County, Growing Solutions Everywhere, reflects both the depth of SFUA's roots in this community and the expanding reach of the regional food system we are building together.

Proceeds from Harvest Moon directly support SFUA's programs: farm coordination, agricultural education, and the distribution infrastructure that moves fresh produce to the communities that need it most.

Why It Matters

Every seat supports the food system

Harvest Moon is SFUA's primary annual fundraiser. It powers the programs that run throughout the year: from the fields to the classrooms to the food banks.

Funds raised support farm coordination and market access, agricultural education programs in schools, distribution logistics that move fresh produce to communities across Orange, Los Angeles, and Riverside counties, and the partnerships with farms and institutions that make all of it possible.

When you take a seat at the table, you are investing in the farmers who grow the food, the students who learn where it comes from, and the neighbors who receive it.
